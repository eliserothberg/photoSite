SameSource is a photographic artist with over two decades of professional experience recording images. SameSource fine art spans both landscape and bodyscape, often exploring human sexuality and its relationship with art. With recent showings from the Musée du Louvre, Paris, France, to Art Basel, Miami Beach, SameSource is becoming an established international presence. Coagula Gallery in Los Angeles has included SameSource in its Ten Top Artists exhibition for 2016 in a show curated by Tulsa Kinney of Artillery Magazine.

SameSource is based in Los Angeles but has origins in the rural Midwest. After studying music and philosophy in a liberal arts education, the artist went to Italy and became immersed in the works of the Renaissance. A return to the US brought the pursuit of graduate work in music and cinema, with an eventual arrival on the west coast via the USC film school. In addition to the full-time pursuit of photographic fine art, SameSource output has included notable works as a recording artist, writer, and filmmaker.

Okay, so what’s with the weird name?

I came across a Henry Miller passage, and it resonated for me. He referred to all creation deriving from the "same source". Artistic expression is the one thing we do that really isn’t about survival or procreation. Therein lies our humanity. I do feel that the creative desire is within all of us, and the reason art has the power to break down barriers is that it all comes from that same place–the source of human expression that compels us to add some meaning to our lives while we are here. By choosing an alias that denies the viewer a context for my gender and ethnic background, I rob them of some preconceived notions when approaching my work. Hence, I borrowed from Miller and shoved the words together to represent the discomfort and truth that can be found in the elimination of personal space in art. While I avoid attaching my birth name to my work for these reasons, I don’t try to hide my identity in person.
